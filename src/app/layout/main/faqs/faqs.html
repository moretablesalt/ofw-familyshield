Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the Migrant Workers and Overseas Filipinos Act (MWOFA) of 1995, as amended?

This is the law that protects Overseas Filipinos and Migrant Workers. It provides adequate and timely social, economic, and legal services to Filipino migrant workers. It affords protection to labor—organized and unorganized—and promotes full employment and equal employment opportunities.

Who is an Overseas Filipino Worker (OFW)?

A Filipino citizen who is about to engage, presently engaged, or has engaged in the past in an activity with compensation:

a) In another country where he/she is not a citizen;
b) On board a vessel navigating foreign seas (excluding government ships used for military or non-commercial purposes);
c) On an installation located offshore or on the high seas.

What is the difference between an OFW and a migrant worker?

They mean the same thing. In the context of the MWOFA of 1995, as amended, the terms OFW and migrant worker are used interchangeably.

What is the difference between an agency-hired OFW and a direct-hire or name-hire OFW?

An OFW is agency-hired if he/she availed the services of a recruitment or manning agency duly authorized by the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE) through the Philippine Overseas Employment Administration (POEA).

An OFW is direct-hired or name-hired if he/she was hired directly by foreign employers such as international organizations, diplomatic corps, or secured employment without the assistance of a recruitment or manning agency.

What is Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance?

The Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance, also known as the Compulsory Insurance Coverage for Agency-Hired Migrant Workers, is an insurance mechanism mandated by law to provide insurance protection for agency-hired OFWs.

Who are covered by the Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance?

This insurance is mandatory for agency-hired OFWs. It is not mandatory for direct-hired, name-hired, or re-hired OFWs, but they may avail of the insurance if they wish.

Is suicide covered by the Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance?

Yes. Coverage starts upon enforcement of the insurance coverage. The usual two-year contestability period in standard insurance contracts does not apply to the Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance.

What are valid reasons for claiming the Repatriation Cost Benefit?

The Repatriation Cost Benefit may be claimed in the following situations:

• Illegal termination by the employer
• Non-payment of salary
• Maltreatment
• Overwork
• Poor living conditions (e.g., no running water, no proper bed)
• Poor working conditions (e.g., non-payment of agreed bonus, no break time)
• Medical reasons

Homesickness, loneliness, laziness, personal problems, criminal offenses, and violation of employer rules are not valid reasons and are not covered.

How do I file a claim for Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance?

To file a claim, the OFW, recruitment agency, or beneficiary must contact Prudential Guarantee at +63 2 8459 4763.

The recruitment agency is ultimately responsible for assisting the OFW and/or beneficiary in claiming benefits.

Where can I file a claim for Agency-Hired OFW Compulsory Insurance?

The OFW or beneficiary must notify the recruitment agency of any covered event. If the recruitment agency cannot be contacted, you may call +63 2 8459 4763 directly.
